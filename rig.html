My PC Specs (and server nonsense)

Why does this exist?

A few people asked about the what and whys of my PC(s), so I thought I'd put them here.

Main Rig: (Built early 2025)

A high-performance build designed to still be money-concsious.
  • CPU: AMD Ryzen 7 9800x3D
    • All around good CPU. Could have upgraded to a 9950, but there was really no point, even if it seems like a good deal nonetheless.
  • CPU Cooler: Thermalright Peerless Assassin
    • What is there to say, it's an exceptional cooler for the price. Cools the 9800x3D perfectly fine. I was considering a Noctua NH-D15 but it's completely outclassed in price-to-performance here.
  • GPU: NVidia GeForce RTX 5080
    • Picked over any of AMDs offerings because AMD doesn't really sell a card comparable to this in performance. I had money to spare, so I decided to grab the best I can get.
  • RAM: 128GB (4x32GB) of DDR5-6000 CL18 sticks from Silicon Power
    • Not much to say about these other than they were cheap for DDR5 and 6000MT/s is fast enough. Yes, I am aware of the performance impacts of 4 sticks of RAM. I do not care.
  • Storage: A Generic 1TB SSD from my old Rig (See Media Server below) and a 2TB Samsung 990
    • Also not much to say here. They work, and they are plenty fast.
  • Motherboard: Gigabyte B850 GAMING WIFI6
    • Picked for it's port selection. Plenty of USB, Ethernet, and Audio to go around. It's a nice mobo.
  • PSU: SeaSonic VERTEX P-1000 80+ Platinum Modular ATX PSU (1000W)
    • 2 Main requirements for this part: 80 Plus certification and SeaSonic made.
    I picked SeaSonic specifically due to that absolutely ballsy move they made to sponsor the PSU Tester for LTT Labs.
    Sponsoring that means they are sure to be tested to their claims and it shows confidence in their products. Also, their warranties are good, and they actually honor them. +üëç for SeaSonic.
  • Case: Fractal Design North XL
    • There were a few requirements for this case:
    • No stupid glass side panel (I leave my PC on at night sometimes, and any RGB in the case prevents me from sleeping.)
    • Good airflow
    • Good looking
    • Ideally, white colored.
    I'm never buying a cheap case again. This case was a dream to build in, roomy and completely ventillated. It ticks all the boxes, and it looks excellent.
    I particularly like the side-panel fan spots, which allowed me to put a few fans for intake to the GPU to keep it nice and cool.
    (All Noctua by the way, I've personally seen these fans go through hell and back and be basically new still, and I love the brown.)
    Front IO is really good (Headphone & Mic, 2x USB-A, 1x USB-C) and the built-in cable management is excellent.
    The only thing I think I would have wanted over this is the Silverstone FLP-02 but it wasn't out when I built it. (nor as I write this, guys, when is this releasing? I WANT MY SLEEPER CASE)
  • Monitor(s): an LG UltraGear 34GS95QE-B 34" 3440x1440@240Hz Ultrawide with HDR True Black 400 and G-Sync/FreeSync, and a few free "good enough" 1080p 16:9 monitors from various places (I've had them for years.) one is mounted vertically.
    • I've been meaning to get a set of monitor arms for the 3-monitor setup I got going here. The Ultrawide is excellent, and I fucking love it. I'm never playing games 16:9 again.
  • Operating System: Arch Linux with KDE Plasma as my desktop environment, and fish for the shell.
    • I could go on and on about linux, but nah. Instead, the silly details of my config:
    No swap - 128GB of RAM is plenty. I considered a paging file or swap partition but it's completely unnessessary with *that* much RAM.
    Cursor has been set to Posy's Cursor, as I like it. Black-on-white FTW. Similarly, the system-wide monospaced font is the one you are reading this page in right now, Iosevka with some customisations.
    I am using yakuake [sic] for a terminal, as it ships with KDE and it's really cool.
  • Extras: A Schiit Audio Magni Unity DAC+Headphone Amp paired with a pair of Custom Cans modded Beyerdynamic DT770s, a Samson Go meteor mic, and a NanoKVM Pro Desk for remote access. The desk itself is a discontinued IKEA Jerker desk.
    Everything here is a hard recommend. Audio stuff sounds great, and the KVM is nice to have. The desk is nice, but again, discontinued.
    Eventually, I plan to build a custom desk. Also, I should make a hardware "Media Volume" slider box and other assorted shit for my PC.

    The Media Server and NAS

    There's nothing special about this PC. It's literally my old rig, save for about 10TB of random ass drives crammed into it.
    It's got a Intel Xeon ES-1650 v3 (about equivalent to a i7-7700K) and a EVGA 1070 for transcoding. 32 GBs of RAM tops off the tower of mediocrity.
    It's "good enough" for my needs and runs Debian without a DE, and I SSH into it. It's running Copyparty for NAS use and Jellyfin on top for media. Caddy provides a reverse-proxy for the services running within. It works well.
